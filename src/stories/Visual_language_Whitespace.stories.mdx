# Whitespace

#### You need more whitespace than you think.

<div style={{ maxWidth: "600px", margin: "25px 0px 25px 50px" }}>
  <p>
    “Fail to the left, fail to the right.”
    <p>– Erik G. Kennedy</p>
  </p>
</div>

<div className="subheading">
  you don't need borders. you need more whitespace.
</div>

<p>
  Whitespace is a unique thing in the sense that it’s the only thing that
  doesn’t add anything to the UI; the only thing you can increase without
  cluttering the UI.
</p>

<ol
  style={{
    fontWeight: "800",
    color: "#838383",
    padding: "25px 25px 25px 55px",
    backgroundColor: "#e1e3e5",
    borderRadius: "5px",
  }}
>
  <li>Start adding whitespace around an element.</li>
  <li>Add until there's definitely too much. That's your upper bound.</li>
  <li>
    Decrease until it’s clearly too little – then you know that’s the lower
    limit.
  </li>
  <li>Iterate between the two values - until there's just the right amount.</li>
</ol>

<p>
  This technique, by the way, applies to a lot of UI work – rather than trying
  to distill the exact pixel value on the first iteration, test on purpose what
  is too much, and what is too little.
</p>
<p>
  Generous whitespace not only looks good but also solves many other problems:
</p>
<p>
  1. Because elements are clearly close/far enough from each other, you don’t
  have to make distinction and grouping with adding borders, which adds no
  information, but increases clutter in UI.
</p>
<p>
  2. You’re making your own life easy – it’s easier avoiding adding too much
  content, because the space available is occupied already - by whitespace.
</p>
